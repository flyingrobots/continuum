# Contract family atlas

- Lane: `cool-ideas`
- Legend: `SOURCE`

## Why now

The shared family story is getting clearer, but it is still mostly reconstructed
from a mix of schema files, design packets, and consumer repos.

An atlas would not replace the schemas or the registry. It would make the
relationships visible:

- authored families
- adjacent families
- emitted artifacts
- runtime emitters
- downstream consumers

## Hill

A maintainer can open one Continuum-local atlas and understand the current
shared-family spine without jumping across half the repos.

## Done looks like

- one local-first atlas view or document exists
- the atlas visually or textually separates:
  - authored families
  - generated artifacts
  - runtime emitters
  - downstream consumers
- the atlas is useful for Wesley, Echo, `warp-ttd`, and app maintainers
- the atlas stays narrow enough to remain factual rather than turning into a
  giant doctrine mural
